Becoming Responsible

Chuba Ikueme

I remember a time when my parents went out, and then, I was doing something (I can't remember what exactly). My siblings were playing with each other. Everything was going well.

Then something happened. My brother broke the TV. I was bothered that he broke the TV, but I knew that I was not going to be punished. At least, I thought so. I told my brother that he was in a lot of trouble.

My parents finally came back, and they found out what happened. Thinking that they would punish my brother, they punished me. I was shocked. My parents told me that the reason they punished me was that I was not watching my brother. They said that if I was watching him, he would not have broken the TV. And then, I was thinking; "Why would I be watching my brother? He's not even the TV!" (LOL).

But now, I understand why my parents did that. It was because they wanted me to be responsible. They left me to look after them, but I did not. I was just doing my own thing. I would say that this is a life lesson. Age comes with responsibility. As you get older, you become in charge. And when you are in charge, you are responsible for everything that happens around you.
