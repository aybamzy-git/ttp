Overnight success doesn't exsist.

Ordinary Kyle

So when my sister was born in 2009, an uncle of mine came to live with us,and he used to write songs. He was like an older brother to me, so I wanted to do everything he did. My dad used to rap as well, so I believe that's what got me started really. Then in 2011, I started uploading acapellas to YouTube.

I faced many challenges when I was just starting out. At the very beginning, my writing ability was not all that spectacular. I had talent, but I was very young so I had to develop the talent that I had.

Another challenge that I had was that I did not know how to record. I was just playing beats off YouTube and recording myself singing over them with my phone.

I got to learn about copyright and how I couldn't monetize any music with a beat that isn't mine. And since I had no capital, I had to learn to make beats, learn to record professionally, and so on.

With all the challenges, it was not easy. I could have given up, but I didn't. The truth is overnight success doesn't exist. You keep at it. With all of it, I decide to stay independent, because I have learnt how shady record labels can be. It won't be easy to just become known. You have to work and keep at it.
